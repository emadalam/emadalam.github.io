Having worked for many startups as a freelancer and currently being part of one as a full-time employee, I can go on telling my experiences and why I like to be part of this continuously evolving culture, but let me keep this very crisp and brief to keep the interest going. I'm summarizing it in a few bullet points and a tagline to each of them, hopefully that will suffice the purpose and convey just enough.

  • Fast Paced

    We live in a a fast-paced world. Walking slows us down.

  • Challenges

    We don’t grow when things are easy; we grow when we face challenges.

  • Complete Ownership

    Taking complete ownership of your outcome by holding no one but yourself responsible for them is the most powerful thing you can do to drive your success.

  • Explore and Experiment

    Explore and Experiment. The world has much to offer, and you have much to give. And every time you try something new, you’ll learn more about yourself.

Like

  • The !dea
    I remember the last time when I was searching for a candidate for my previous organization and scrutinizing thousands of resumes, taking at least 2-3 interviews everyday. Trust me it was so painful and disappointing when the candidates used to bluff so much in their resumes and the pain of interviewing them on phone just to know whether they are even worth calling for a face-off. I started preparing mini-projects and set of questions to send them out and get the initial worthiness. I so wished I stumbled upon something like codility to ease the whole process.

Dislikes

  • The font-end stack
    After a li'l bit of fiddling around the website, I see that the whole front-end stack at codility is a bit of a mess and using stale age old front-end technologies. No offense, but this is something you guys @codility will have to agree to. The front-end seems to be a sidelined stuff at the development center and looks so tightly coupled with the back-end stack. I, being so passionate and sentimental about front-end development, I really feel hurt. I so wish I could change all this and I mean it.
  • Disconnect
    This was a serious turn off for me. The fact that each of the pages/sections across the website felt as if I landed on a complete different website altogether, which in my opinion, isn't the best thing to do, especially when the actual product is so good, why to turn people off by giving an unprofessional feel.
  • Others
    Few notable mentions are, there isn't any CDN serving the assets, no localization, no internationalization of the content, only available in English, are you guys not targeting the international market? no proper API documentation, etc.
